# Lima's Kitchen — PRODUCT

register: brand

## Product purpose
A one-page ordering site for a home-based Bengali kitchen in Welling (DA16, London).
Friends and their wider circle (mosque community, families) browse party food, place an
order for collection, and pay cash in person. No online payment. The owner is phoned to
confirm every order. It is a small, personal, one-woman operation. Ordering here should
feel like ordering from someone you know, not from a chain or a delivery app.

## Users
- **Friends & community** ordering party food for gatherings (dawats, Eid, family events).
  Many are Bengali-speaking; many order on their phones. They value trust, warmth, and a
  personal touch over slick convenience.
- **The owner (Lima)**: not technical. Manages every order from her phone. Needs each order
  as a notification with full details, plus a simple list she can act on.

## Brand & tone
- Name: **Lima's Kitchen** / **লিমা'স রান্নাঘর**. Both scripts are first-class.
- Warm, personal, trustworthy, premium-but-homely. A talented home cook's table, not a
  restaurant brand. Plain, warm copy in sentence case. No marketing fluff, no exclamation
  spam, no "authentic taste of Bengal" cliché.
- The owner's first-person note is the emotional centre. The personal angle is the point.

## Strategic principles
1. **Bilingual to the core.** English and Bengali, full parity: nav, buttons, story, menu,
   form, messages, footer. A toggle switches everything and is remembered for the visit.
   Bengali mode is its own cohesive look, not swapped text.
2. **Trust before transaction.** Show allergens on every dish. Be honest about lead time,
   collection-only, cash-on-collection, and the confirming phone call.
3. **Her hands, not a logo.** The identity comes from Bengali domestic craft (nakshi kantha
   running-stitch, alpona linework), not restaurant tropes.
4. **Never lose an order.** Every order is stored permanently and reaches her.

## Anti-references (do NOT look like these)
- Deliveroo / Uber Eats / JustEat: transactional, cold, card-grid, aggressive.
- Generic "Indian restaurant" sites: saffron-on-black, mandala wallpaper, gold gradients.
- SaaS landing pages: hero-metric templates, identical feature-card grids, glassmorphism.

## Reflex-reject (avoid the obvious answer)
- First-order reflex: turmeric-orange on cream + a mandala. Rejected.
- Second-order reflex: deep-green + gold "premium ethnic". Rejected.
- Chosen instead: terracotta + deep teal (clay oven & pottery glaze) on warm oatmeal paper,
  one marigold accent, craft linework from kantha/alpona. Bengali mode warms and gilds.
